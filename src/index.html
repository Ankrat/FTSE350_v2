Our interactive chart displays a sector-by-sector view of the FTSE350 Cyber Governance Health Check responses, on what we believe to be the five most important issues.

The FTSE350 Cyber Governance Health Check is a Government initiative that assesses and reports levels of cyber security awareness and preparedness across the UK’s largest organisations.

Please feel free to explore the sector trends, and answer the questions yourself within the benchmarking tool to see how you compare to those that participated in this initiative.

The 2014 Cyber Governance Health Check (‘The Tracker’) assesses and reports levels of cyber security awareness and preparedness across the FTSE 350, from a governance perspective. Under the Government’s National Cyber Security Programme and Strategy, the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills is working in collaboration with business advisory firms to develop and manage the Tracker process. This tool gives a comparison of your company’s answers to a subset of the FTSE 350 Cyber Governance Health Check Survey in 2014 and 2013 with the aggregate results for both years.

This exercise is not intended to provide an opinion about the effectiveness of security controls at individual participants nor the index as a whole.

The FTSE 350 is an index based on market capitalisation with entries and exits happening on a quarterly basis and as such it is not necessarily representative of all industry sectors. Surveys are prone to non-response and self-selection bias. For this survey, the response rate in 2013 was 62% versus 31% in 2014. The questionnaire was open during September and October in both years. The data was processed and stored by a third party selected by the UK Government (Department for Business, Innovation and Skills) – Netready Ltd. The names of participants in the survey are confidential.
